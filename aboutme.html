Professional Experience

I have gained hands-on experience across health policy, political campaigns, and international diplomacy. At the Schroeder Center for Health Policy, I manage social media content to increase engagement. As a Fall Campaign Fellow for Mark Warner for Virginia, I created outreach materials and supported local campaign events. At the Embassy of Spain, I tracked legislation, coordinated diplomatic events, and assisted the Consulate Section. As a Legislative Intern in Senator Tim Kaine’s office, I drafted briefing materials, managed constituent communications, and led Capitol tours. These roles have strengthened my skills in research, communication, and policy analysis.

Personal Life

Outside of academics, I stay active and balanced by attending Pure Barre regularly, going on walks around Colonial Williamsburg, and spending time with friends. I also enjoy studying at Aromas, soaking in the campus atmosphere while working on projects. On campus, I am involved in my sorority, serving as Vice President of Recruitment Information in 2024, and I participate in the sailing club, competing in regattas through the MAISA conference. In my free time I enjoy reading and have set a goal to read 30 books in 2026. These activities help me stay energized, inspired, and ready to take on new challenges.

Global Footprint

I have always loved to travel, and in Spring 2025, I had the incredible opportunity to study abroad in Sevilla, Spain, which allowed me to explore much of Europe. Since then, I have visited 19 countries, with 15 of them visited during 2025 alone. Each place has offered unique experiences and cultural insights, from the stunning coastline of Nice, France, my favorite city, to the breathtaking landscapes of Austria, my favorite country. I also have a special fondness for Spain and Portugal, whose vibrant cultures, architecture, and cuisine continue to inspire me. Traveling has not only broadened my perspective but also fueled my curiosity about the world and its diverse people.
